# Cosmetic Insights: Navigating Cosmetics Trends and Consumer Insights with Tableau

## Publishing

### Description

After completing the dashboard and validating the visualizations, the workbook is published to **Tableau Public** to make it accessible online. Publishing enables users to share interactive dashboards, embed them into websites, and showcase their data visualization projects with a wider audience.

### 1. Prepare Your Dashboard or Story

Before publishing, ensure that the dashboard or story is complete and functions as expected.

- Verify that all charts, filters, and interactive elements work correctly.
- Remove any unnecessary worksheets, dashboards, or unused data sources to reduce the workbook size.
- Review the layout, formatting, and titles to ensure a professional presentation.
- Confirm that all calculations and visualizations display accurate results.

---

### 2. Sign in to Tableau Public

To publish the workbook, sign in to your Tableau Public account.

- Open **Tableau Desktop**.
- Navigate to **File → Save to Tableau Public**.
- If you are not already signed in, a login window will appear.
- Enter your **Tableau Public** credentials or create a new account if you do not already have one.

---

### 3. Save and Publish

Once signed in, publish the workbook to Tableau Public.

- Enter a meaningful name for the workbook.
- Click **Save** to upload the workbook.
- Tableau will publish the dashboard or story to your Tableau Public profile.
- Wait until the upload process is completed successfully.

---

### 4. View Your Published Dashboard or Story

After publishing, Tableau automatically opens the workbook in your default web browser.

From the published page, you can:

- View the interactive dashboard or story online.
- Share the dashboard using its public URL.
- Embed the dashboard into a website or blog.
- Update the workbook by republishing newer versions.
- Manage the visibility settings of your published workbook through your Tableau Public profile.

---

### Outcome

Publishing the dashboard on **Tableau Public** makes the project accessible from any device with an internet connection. It allows users, stakeholders, and recruiters to interact with the visualizations, explore insights, and share the dashboard through a public link or embedded web page.
